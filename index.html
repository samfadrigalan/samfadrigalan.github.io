About

Hello, my name is Samantha Fadrigalan! I am a rising junior in Computer Science, with a minor in Math, at Louisiana State University. Geaux Tigers!

I fell— or was pushed— into Computer Science in my junior year of high school. One of my math teachers tried to convince me to take her C++ class during the spring. I refused. However, that same teacher also happened to be my advisor, and had the power to schedule my classes. And so I had planned on bypassing her and drop the class the first week of the spring semester. Needless to say, I never did it.

I enjoy enjoy volunteering and organizing outreach events. I'm a member of Society of Peer Mentors (SPM) at LSU, and I get to mentor local high school students during FIRST robotics season. I also co-founded a hackathon at LSU, GeauxHack, with tons of help from SPM. I'm also an officer of the very new Women in Computer Science (WICS) organization at LSU. A bunch of us from SPM and WICS are planning a program to teach CS using Arduinos to secondary school girls. I also recently became president of Association of Computing Machinery (ACM) at LSU. If you would like to come join us for an ACM meeting, inquire about GeauxHack, or talk about anything from what I have mentioned, feel free to contact me!

This summer, I will have my very first internship as a Consultant intern at Pariveda Solutions in Houston. I'm very excited start.

Aside from all things CS, I love music. I play guitar, piano, and sing, unafraid of how I sound. I often treat my musically talented friends as my personal band. I enjoy tasting new foods. I love taking walks outside if it's just about 60°F and the sky is a shade of orange. I love learning about other cultures. I'm recentely entering a Marvel phase (MCU). I might delve into the comic books soon.

If you'd like to come visit an ACM meeting, inquire about GeauxHack, or anything above, feel free to contact me. If you would just like to say hi, you can contact me too! I love meeting people. If you want to look at my code for this website, checkout my Github. Thank you for reading this far. :)
